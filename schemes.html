Government Support Schemes

In addition to charities, a number of government programmes provide financial and nutritional assistance to families with children and other vulnerable groups. These schemes can help with the cost of food and access to healthy meals.

Healthy Start

The Healthy Start scheme offers a pre‑paid card to people who are more than 10 weeks pregnant or have a child under four. If you’re eligible you’ll receive a payment every four weeks which can be used to buy plain liquid cow’s milk, fresh, frozen or tinned fruit and vegetables, pulses and infant formula【553929988290278†L41-L56】. The card also allows you to collect Healthy Start vitamins for you and vitamin drops for your baby【553929988290278†L57-L62】. See the NHS Healthy Start website for eligibility criteria and how to apply.

Free School Meals

Children whose families receive certain means‑tested benefits (such as Income Support, income‑based Jobseeker’s Allowance, income‑related Employment and Support Allowance or Universal Credit with an annual net income below £7,400) may be entitled to free school meals【725033235231495†L111-L124】. Children in reception, year 1 and year 2 automatically receive free school meals in state‑funded schools【725033235231495†L139-L147】. Eligible pupils may also qualify for holiday food vouchers and access to the Holiday Activities and Food Programme via their local council【725033235231495†L98-L102】.

Holiday Activities and Food (HAF) Programme

The HAF programme provides grant funding for local authorities in England to co‑ordinate free holiday clubs for children eligible for free school meals. The funding supports healthy meals, enriching activities and childcare during Easter, summer and Christmas holidays【70504747177042†L90-L169】. Contact your local council to find out about activities and how to access them.
